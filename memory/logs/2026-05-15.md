### Idea 1 — LiquidLine (executable)

**Thesis** (1 sentence): The Polymarket liquidity-reward program pays a documented edge to anyone running a two-sided quoting bot, but ~zero retail traders run one — package the bot, the strategy library, and the reward-farming logic as a one-click managed service and skim a share of P&L.

**ICP** (role + trigger event): Crypto-native retail trader with $5k–$50k idle stablecoin balance who just got rate-limited by Aave / Pendle yields below 5% and is searching for higher-yield deployment that isn't directional exposure.

**Wedge** (first 12 months): Single-product: hosted Polymarket LP bot, ten curated strategies (sports/politics/crypto-duration), automatic reward-tier optimization, real-time P&L dashboard. No backtesting UI, no custom strategies, no Kalshi yet.

**Pain evidence** (2+ permalinks):
- "The space is incredibly underdeveloped compared to traditional crypto markets… maybe one or two other bots providing liquidity across most markets." — https://dev.to/benjamin_martin_749c1d57f/-building-a-polymarket-liquidity-maker-bot-in-python-crypto-5m-1d-markets-349p
- "Professional market makers from traditional finance won't touch new markets — there's too much friction and uncertainty, but individual developers and traders will if given the right tools." — https://news.polymarket.com/p/automated-market-making-on-polymarket

**Monetization**: 20% performance fee on net LP P&L, $0 base. Target gross margin 80%+ (infra costs ~$2/user/month on small VPS; CLOB API is free). Per-user unit econ: $5k deployed × ~15% APY = $750/yr → $150/yr take ≈ break-even at 7 users on a $1k/mo solo overhead.

**Distribution** (specific channel + CAC estimate): (a) Sponsored Top 20 Whale Wallet Movements megathread on r/Polymarket — direct CAC ~$30–80; (b) Inbound from a public "Polymarket LP leaderboard" dashboard (free SEO bait that ranks today's best-paying markets, gated upgrade to autotrade them); (c) Crypto-yield aggregator Telegram groups (DeFi Llama Discord #yields, Stable Summit channel). Estimated blended CAC $50–100.

**Moat** (what compounds): Internal database of which markets/timeframes/spread bands historically paid the most in liquidity rewards — gets richer with every user's executed trades. Workflow lock-in: users hand over a managed Polymarket sub-account; switching cost is non-trivial.

**Why now (2026)**: Polymarket's reward program scaled in 2025, HIP-4 launch on Hyperliquid in April 2026 doubled the addressable LP surface, and Polymarket US opening to American retail (currently in beta with a waitlist as of May 2026) is about to triple end-user count without any matching MM growth.

**MVP test** (2 weeks): Ship a single-strategy bot (BTC 1-hour duration markets, the highest-volume crypto-duration category), onboard 5 pilots from r/Polymarket DMs with $1k each. Metric: ≥3 of 5 see positive net P&L after fees over 14 days AND ≥3 of 5 agree to a 20% perf-fee paid subscription.

**Kill criteria** (numeric): <10 paying users at $50+/month MRR-equivalent in performance fees within 60 days → kill. <$50k cumulative AUM by day 90 → kill.

**Expansion** (what if it works): Add Kalshi LP (different reward mechanics, requires KYC pipe), then Hyperliquid HIP-4 outcome markets — by then you own the "prediction-market yield" category and can lift fees toward 25%.

### Idea 2 — Resolva (ambitious)

**Thesis** (1 sentence): Prediction markets resolve via a manipulable oracle (UMA) and traders increasingly distrust outcomes — sell an **insurance overlay** that pays out when a market settles contrary to a credible truth-source consensus, and you become the trust layer the category currently lacks.

**ICP** (role + trigger event): Polymarket whale wallet (>$50k cumulative volume, identifiable on-chain) who just took a position on a contested-resolution market (elections, geopolitical events, M&A, sports replay calls) and remembers losing money on a previously-disputed market like the March 2025 Ukraine mineral-deal flip.

**Wedge** (first 12 months): Per-market resolution-insurance contracts: trader pays a 0.5–3% premium of position notional; if UMA's resolution diverges from a pre-defined credible truth-source (AP wire, official government statement, named regulator), Resolva pays the position value. Start with a curated whitelist of ~30 high-volume contested-category markets per week.

**Pain evidence** (2+ permalinks):
- "Polymarket is facing mounting scrutiny over its resolution process after multiple high-stakes markets resolved contrary to widely reported facts. The controversy centers on the platform's reliance on UMA's optimistic oracle… alarmed traders." — https://laikalabs.ai/news/polymarket-uma-oracle-resolution-disputes-scrutiny
- "A UMA tycoon allegedly used concentrated voting power to settle a $7 million contract falsely… cast 5 million UMA tokens through three accounts, representing 25% of total votes." — https://orochi.network/blog/oracle-manipulation-in-polymarket-2025

**Monetization**: 0.5–3% premium per insured position, dynamic by category (elections > sports > weather). Target loss ratio <40% based on observed Polymarket dispute frequency (~1% of high-volume markets in 2025). Gross margin 60% after reinsurance + on-chain settlement costs.

**Distribution** (specific channel + CAC estimate): (a) Direct on-chain outreach — top 500 Polymarket wallets are public, send a signed message + 0.01-USDC airdrop with the offer; CAC <$5 per touch, expected reply rate ~3%; (b) Embedded "insure this trade" widget on whale-tracking sites (Polywhaler, PolyTracker) via rev-share — CAC ~$30 effective; (c) Sponsor the r/Polymarket Top Whale Wallet Movements megathread. Blended CAC $40–100 for a customer averaging $500+ premium/year.

**Moat** (what compounds): (1) Actuarial dataset — every priced premium teaches the model which categories/resolvers/sources diverge; competitors need months of resolved markets to reach parity. (2) Reinsurance relationships — first mover signs the LPs willing to underwrite this risk and locks them into exclusivity. (3) Trust brand — once a few high-profile payouts hit Crypto Twitter, the category becomes "Resolva-insured" by default.

**Why now (2026)**: The 2025 UMA governance attack and follow-on contested resolutions made oracle risk a top-3 trader complaint just as Polymarket US, Kalshi, and Hyperliquid HIP-4 are courting *less crypto-native* users who are even less willing to eat oracle risk. CFTC's clarity on prediction-market legitimacy through 2025 opens reinsurance markets that wouldn't touch this in 2023.

**MVP test** (2 weeks): Hand-quote insurance on the next 5 disputed-category markets (e.g. anything involving subjective political outcomes), back the float yourself ($25k bankroll), sell directly via DM to 50 top wallets. Metric: ≥10 policies sold AND positive expected value (premiums collected > expected payouts at 1.2× loss ratio).

**Kill criteria** (numeric): <50 policies sold in 90 days OR realized loss ratio >70% across first 100 policies → kill. <$500k cumulative notional insured by day 120 → kill.

**Expansion** (what if it works): Insurance for Kalshi and Hyperliquid HIP-4 (different resolution mechanics, but same trust problem); then "resolution-as-a-service" white-label for new prediction-market launches that can't yet afford their own oracle; ultimately a parallel-oracle product where Resolva's truth-source consensus becomes its own settlement layer.

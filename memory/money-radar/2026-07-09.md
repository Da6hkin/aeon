## Ideas — ranked fastest first

### Idea 1 — CloseBot for Bookkeepers  ·  first $ in ~10–14 days  ·  build effort: S

**The move.** Sell a done-for-you "monthly close in half the time" service to small bookkeeping/CPA firms (5–20 client base). You run the agent stack (OfficeCLI + a custom SKILL.md pack for reconciliations, flux analysis, statement generation). They send raw workpapers, you deliver closed books + Excel-native model. Priced as recurring, not per-job.

**Who pays, and why now.** Owner-operators of small bookkeeping firms drowning post-tax-season, unable to hire (83% talent shortage), tired of "AI tools" that are chatbots. They already pay $2–5k/mo to a junior. A cheaper, faster, higher-quality substitute lands immediately.

**Signals behind it.** OfficeCLI v1.0.134 today (`github-trending.md`); accounting demand quotes above; skill format portable across 20+ runtimes; skills-as-service is where the money actually is per agent37.

**Fastest path to first dollar.** Cold-DM 30 firm owners on LinkedIn + r/Accounting + r/Bookkeeping over one weekend. Offer: "I'll do your next month-end close for **free**; if you like it, $497 setup + $299/mo after." One free run = case study. Three free runs = 1–2 paying by day 14.

**Price & math.** $299/mo × 5 clients month 1 = **$1,495 MRR** (~$18k ARR pace) with headroom to $499/mo once value is proven. Add one setup fee here or there.

**Distribution.** LinkedIn Sales Navigator filter (accounting/bookkeeping, 2–20 employees, US small metros). Same message to r/Bookkeeping and r/Accounting. NOT content marketing — direct outreach only.

**Why it's non-obvious.** Every AI-for-accountants pitch is **software** (SaaS, Chrome extension). The one thing small firms will actually buy in 2026 is **a person + agents** — a productized service. Everyone building SaaS is competing for the "won't move beyond pilot" segment; you're serving the "we just want it done" segment that's actively opening its wallet.

**Kill criteria.** <2 paying clients within 30 days of first DM sent, or churn >30% by month 2.

**Who pays, and why now.** Indie developers and small agencies shipping MVPs in days. They already pay for Cursor ($20/mo) and Claude Code ($20/mo) — a $29 one-time pack that saves 6 hours is a no-brainer. Marketplaces monetize via Stripe Connect (70/30 split, instant payout).

### Wildcard — SkillOps: the layer selling shovels to skill authors

The strangest intersection today: **top skill authors earn $500–3k/mo but the median earns <$50** — a barbell distribution created entirely by discovery/curation on 8 marketplaces with wildly different rules. That means the tooling to *survive* as a skill author is worth more than most skills.

**The move.** A tiny SaaS (or even a Notion-templated service) that: (1) cross-lists a single SKILL.md across all 4–8 major marketplaces from one dashboard, (2) tracks unified earnings + downloads, (3) suggests marketplace-specific tweaks (titles, keywords, screenshots) based on what's ranking that week. Charge $19/mo flat OR 15% of tracked skill revenue.

**Buyer.** Skill authors already earning something, terrified of leaving money on the table.

**Why it might work.** Classic picks-and-shovels play with fresh infra. Zero direct competition today. Every marketplace wants more skills, so partnership APIs are gettable. And this is the exact kind of "boring tool that serves all the X-chasers" play the skill file explicitly names.

**Risk (honest).** Total addressable market is small — maybe 2–5k active paid skill authors globally by year-end. Even 10% conversion at $19/mo caps at ~$4–10k MRR. Fastest path is real, ceiling is not "life-changing." Also has platform-dependency risk if a marketplace closes its API. Rank it **variance**, not "the plan" — but it's the one idea in this brief that most people would not spot.

**Kill criteria.** Cannot pull a working integration from 3 of the top marketplaces within 2 weeks → shelve.

**Who pays, and why now.** Founder or head of finance at a 5–40-person AI-native SaaS on flat or hybrid pricing whose last-month LLM invoice grew ≥50% MoM without matching revenue. ### Wildcard — AgentLens

The strangest intersection today: **the arXiv paper on "Onchain LM Agent Controls" quietly documents 3,505 real-money trading agents with 7.5M invocations at 99.9% on-chain settlement** — meaning there is already a publicly-visible dataset of real-money AI-agent trades on Base that nobody has productized. Meanwhile, `CocoaBench` says SOTA agents fail 55% of tasks. Meanwhile, retail crypto users are constantly asking "which agent should I trust with my USDC?" and the only answer today is "look at Virtuals' leaderboard" (which measures market cap of the *agent token*, not ROI of the *agent's trades*).

**The move.** A public real-time leaderboard of the top on-chain trading agents by verified ROI + risk-adjusted returns, drawn from the on-chain transaction history of the 3,505-agent cohort (all public data). Free leaderboard as the marketing wedge. Paid tier: $19/mo for alerts when your favorite agent takes a new position, real-time position-following webhook, and per-agent risk score derived from CocoaBench-style adversarial testing.

**Buyer.** Retail crypto users who already trust *something* on Base to run their bag and want a better filter than "vibes on X." Small-fund managers looking for signal without licensing a Bloomberg terminal.

**Why it might work.** Classic "boring tool that serves all the X-chasers" from the skill file. On-chain data is free. The 3,505-agent cohort exists as a real dataset. Follower economies compound (Uniswap V3 LP followers, Nansen smart-money wallets — same shape). Non-obvious because everyone building on Base is building agents, not a filter FOR agents.

**Risk (honest).** (a) **Regulatory:** monetizing "follow this agent's trades" borders on Investment Adviser Act territory in the US — needs a compliance-safe UI framing ("trade replication tool" not "investment advice"). (b) **Platform-dependency:** if Coinbase-for-Agents or Bankr ship this natively, competition kills it. (c) **Signal quality:** agent ROI is noisy over short windows; leaderboards drift. (d) **TAM ceiling:** the follower crypto market is real but capped at maybe $2–5M ARR before the same regulatory questions get asked louder. Label as **variance**, not "the plan." But it is the one idea in this brief that would surprise a room of Base builders.

**Kill criteria.** Cannot pull clean ROI data for 100+ agents from public on-chain history within 2 weeks → data claim was inflated, shelve. <500 free-tier signups within 30 days of Show HN + Farcaster launch → the "which agent should I trust" question isn't as hot as I think, kill. First cease-and-desist from CFTC/SEC/FinCEN → hard stop, ship the tool as pure data (no follow button).

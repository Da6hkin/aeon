Brain's Hidden Defense Against Alzheimer's Discovered UCLA & UCSF researchers used CRISPR screening on human neurons to identify CRL5SOCS4, a protein complex that tags toxic tau for destruction. Neurons with higher CRL5SOCS4 levels survived better in Alzheimer's brain tissue. The study also linked mitochondrial dysfunction to a specific tau fragment matching a known Alzheimer's blood biomarker. Source: sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/03/260303145730.htm 2. ChatGPT as Therapist? Brown University Flags Serious Ethical Risks New Brown University research (Mar 2) finds that AI chatbots like ChatGPT, even when prompted to act as therapists, routinely violate core ethical standards of clinical practice. As millions turn to LLMs for mental health advice, the study highlights risks of AI-based pseudo-therapy at scale. Source: sciencedaily.com (Mar 2, 2026) 3. AI Maps Hidden Chemical Changes Across the Alzheimer's Brain Rice University scientists produced the first complete dye-free molecular atlas of an Alzheimer's brain by combining laser-based imaging with machine learning. The atlas reveals chemical changes beyond amyloid plaques, opening new avenues for understanding disease progression. Source: sciencedaily.com (Mar 1, 2026) 4. Accelerated TMS: Depression Relief in Just 5 Days A UCLA study shows that compressing 6 weeks of transcranial magnetic stimulation into 5 days of intensive treatment delivers comparable relief for treatment-resistant depression. Some patients who didn't improve immediately showed a 36% drop in depression scores 2–4 weeks later. Source: sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/02/260224023103.htm 5. New Brain Barrier Discovered at Choroid Plexus Published in Nature Neuroscience, Verhaege et al. describe a previously overlooked brain barrier at the base of the choroid plexus—the first new brain barrier discovery since the blood-brain barrier was identified in the 19th century.
